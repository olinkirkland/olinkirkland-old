About

2014 Edinburgh and Berlin

Always interested in making pixels move, I started my career in 2014 after studying Computer Science with a Game Design concentration at George Mason University in Virginia. After working for a year as a web developer for the university, I chose to explore Europe by moving to Edinburgh and then Berlin, practicing freelance graphic design and application development to fund my travels. I learned a lot about staying on top of a variety of projects, managing my time, and interacting amicably with demanding clients.

2015 Virginia

I moved back to the United States after a year to look for a software job where I could learn and grow my technical skills. While I hunted for the next rung in my career, I worked the summer at the day camp "Renaissance Child", teaching children basic programming and engineering.

After the summer ended, I decided on a job offer from an exhibit company in Houston, Texas that needed a programmer to develop touch-screen applications for a new Energy Hall in the Houston Museum of Natural Science.

2015-2018 Texas

During my time with Paul Bernhard Exhibits I wrote code for a wide variety of applications and games, working with LEAP Motion, Apache Flex, networked interactive applications, Arduino, 4K video streaming, and ArcGIS (and custom mapping solutions). I also had the opportunity to work closely with curators, researchers, and content creators at the museum.

It was a demanding but rewarding role. You can view some of the applications I developed on my project page.

2018-2020 Regensburg

The Energy Hall project was completed and the exhibit company was having trouble finding new projects. I decided to seek a new job in Germany, which I had missed after living there during my time freelancing.

That year, my wife and I moved to Germany and I joined the front-end team at Dallmeier GmbH in Regensburg to work on a large security application using Actionscript 3 and the Apache Flex framework.

At Dallmeier GmbH, I had the opportunity to work closely with skilled senior developers and learned a great deal about code structure and good practice in object oriented programming.

2020-2021 Cologne

I began to develop small hobby projects in my free time. As time went on, I felt that it was "now or never" to explore a possible career path in the game industry. I moved to Cologne and have been working at Bright Future as a Haxe/OpenFl Front-end game developer on the web game, Rail Nation.
